Home page

Here are the top 10 unforgettable Disney Night dances on Dancing with the Stars that capture the magic, drama, and charm of Disney like never before. From Riker Lynch’s fierce Pirates of the Caribbean Paso Doble to Simone Biles’ powerful Moana Contemporary, these routines blend storytelling, choreography, and jaw-dropping performances that left viewers spellbound. Dive into this list to relive the spectacular nights where contestants truly embodied Disney heroes, from Milo Manheim’s superhero Quickstep as Mr. Incredible to Zendaya’s elegant waltz as Belle from Beauty and the Beast. Discover what made each dance stand out in a night of pure Disney enchantment! Discover the top 10 most impactful BYU devotionals that have inspired and uplifted generations. From Brad Wilcox's powerful message on grace, to Jeffrey R. Holland's enduring wisdom on faith and perseverance, these talks address life's challenges, personal growth, and spiritual transformation. Whether it’s Lawrence E. Corbridge’s call to stand firm in faith, or Russell M. Nelson’s insight into the balance of love and law, each devotional offers profound guidance for navigating life’s journey. These talks, filled with hope, accountability, and spiritual insight, are must-reads for anyone seeking deeper understanding and strength in their faith.

Top 10 Dancing with The Stars Disney Night Dances

Riker Lynch and Allison Holker – Pirates of the Caribbean – Paso Doble (Season 20) This dance was explosive, capturing the intensity and drama of Pirates of the Caribbean. Riker embodied Jack Sparrow’s swagger and confidence, while Allison’s choreography was sharp, bold, and exciting, making this Paso Doble a fan favorite.

Simone Biles and Sasha Farber – Moana – Contemporary (Season 24) Simone’s athleticism and grace shone in this performance. The dance captured Moana’s adventurous spirit and strength, with Simone perfectly embodying the character’s journey. The contemporary style allowed for fluidity and raw emotion that made it especially moving.

Jordan Fisher and Lindsey Arnold – Moana – Foxtrot (Season 25) Jordan’s Foxtrot was seamless and sophisticated. It combined Disney magic with technical excellence, and his natural chemistry with Lindsey made the dance feel as effortless and enchanting as the movie itself.

Milo Manheim & Witney Carson – The Incredibles – Quickstep (Season 27) This Quickstep was energetic, fun, and full of character, embodying The Incredibles’ family dynamics and superhero theme. Milo’s enthusiasm and Witney’s inventive choreography made this dance unforgettable and full of personality.

James Hinchcliffe and Sharna Burgess – The Jungle Book – Quickstep (Season 23) James surprised everyone with his lighthearted, playful take on Baloo. The choreography was fast-paced and humorous, showcasing his versatility and ability to tell a story through dance.

Alfonso Ribeiro and Witney Carson – Alice in Wonderland – Foxtrot (Season 19) As the first Disney Night dance ever, this routine set the bar high. Alfonso’s charisma and Witney’s whimsical choreography perfectly captured the quirky charm of Alice in Wonderland.

Zendaya and Val Chmerkovskiy – Beauty and the Beast – Viennese Waltz (Season 16) Zendaya’s waltz captured the romance and elegance of Beauty and the Beast. Her graceful performance brought the classic Disney story to life, earning praise for its beauty and fluidity.

Lindsey Stirling and Mark Ballas – Pinocchio – Jazz (Season 25) Lindsey’s Jazz number was a creative and whimsical take on Pinocchio. Her movements were quirky and precise, channeling the iconic character’s innocence and joy while showcasing her musicality.

Nyle DiMarco and Peta Murgatroyd – Tarzan – Samba (Season 22) Nyle’s samba embodied Tarzan’s wild, free-spirited nature. His connection with Peta and his ability to perform with grace and rhythm (despite being deaf) made this dance both powerful and inspiring.

Laurie Hernandez and Val Chmerkovskiy – Finding Dory – Foxtrot (Season 23) Laurie’s Foxtrot was as delightful and buoyant as Dory herself. The dance was light-hearted, well-executed, and full of personality, showcasing Laurie’s charm and skill.

Top 10 BYU Devotionals

“His Grace is Sufficient” Brad Wilcox Wilcox explains that grace is not only about being saved "after all we can do," but also about transforming us throughout our lives. He emphasizes that grace is an active force, empowering us to change, rather than merely forgiving us. This message resonates deeply for its clarity on grace’s role in everyday life.

“Stand Forever” Lawrence E. Corbridge Corbridge addresses doubt and faith, urging listeners to hold onto foundational beliefs, especially when facing questions or uncertainties. He teaches that some truths are so profound that they merit unwavering commitment, making it a powerful talk on staying firm in one's beliefs during times of trial.

“Be 100 Percent Responsible” Lynn G Robbins Robbins emphasizes personal accountability, suggesting that taking full responsibility for one’s actions is essential to personal growth. He contrasts blaming with accountability, providing practical advice for living a purposeful, morally responsible life—a theme that resonates strongly in the BYU community.

“Lifelong Conversation” Dale G. Renlund Renlund discusses conversion as an ongoing process that extends throughout one’s life, not just a one-time event. By focusing on continuous spiritual growth, he inspires listeners to view conversion as a journey requiring dedication and daily effort.

"Cast Not Away Therefore Your Confidence" - Jeffrey R. Holland In this memorable devotional, Holland encourages perseverance through trials. He shares that often after a spiritual experience or personal revelation, we face opposition. His advice to remain confident despite difficulties has made this a widely cited and comforting talk for those going through hard times.

“Of Souls, Symbols, and Sacraments” Jeffrey R Holland Holland explores the sanctity of intimacy within marriage, portraying it as a symbol of unity with eternal significance. This talk is valued for its perspective on personal relationships and the sacredness of marriage, making it relevant for young adults.

"Remember Lot’s Wife" - Jeffrey R. Holland Holland’s powerful message here is about moving forward without dwelling on past mistakes or regrets. By referencing Lot’s wife looking back, he emphasizes the importance of focusing on the future and letting go of what cannot be changed, offering hope for those who feel burdened by the past.

For Times of Trouble -Jeffrey R Holland This talk encourages resilience and faith in the face of trials. Holland reassures listeners that divine help is always near, providing comfort and encouragement for those struggling with challenges. It’s cherished for its uplifting message during difficult moments.

The Love and Laws of God - Russell M. Nelson Nelson discusses the balance between God’s love and His laws, teaching that both are crucial to understanding His character and His expectations for us. This devotional addresses the importance of both mercy and obedience, making it a deeply doctrinal and impactful talk.

We Need an Endowment - Anthony Sweat Sweat emphasizes the need to seek spiritual power through temple worship and the endowment. He explains the symbolism and significance of temple ordinances, encouraging a commitment to temple attendance. His insights resonate as many seek deeper spiritual strength.
